Gupta vs Mauryan Administration: Centralization to Decentralization

UPSC 2020 Q3(a), 2017 Q2(b), 2015 Q3(a), 2013 Q2(b). Don’t write two separate essays. Write one integrated comparison. Here’s the 7-min framework.

You see “Compare Mauryan and Gupta administration” in the paper.

You write Page 1: Mauryan admin. King, Mantriparishad, 27 Adhyakshas, provinces, districts...

Page 2: Gupta admin. King, feudalism, land grants, local autonomy...

You get 8/20.

Why? Because you wrote two essays. UPSC wants one integrated comparison. Similarities, differences, and transition.

The examiner’s rubric: Can you show how Gupta admin was a decline from Mauryan centralization to feudal decentralization? If yes: 14+/20. If you write two separate notes: 8/20.

PYQ Pattern: UPSC Always Asks “Comparison”, Never “Description”

2020 Q3(a): “Compare the administrative systems of Mauryas and Guptas.” — Note: “Compare” = similarities + differences.
2017 Q2(b): “Mauryan administration was highly centralized while Gupta was decentralized.” Comment. — Note: “Comment” = analyze the statement.
2015 Q3(a): “Discuss the changes in administration from Mauryan to Gupta period.” — Note: “Changes” = transition.
2013 Q2(b): “Feudalism in Gupta period marked decline from Mauryan bureaucracy.” Examine.

Never asked: “Describe Mauryan administration.” “Describe Gupta administration.”

The Core Difference: Centralization vs Decentralization

Aspect Mauryan (321-185 BCE) Gupta (320-550 CE)
Nature Highly centralized bureaucracy. King direct control. Decentralized feudalism. Land grants, local autonomy.
King Chakravartin. Absolute. Dhamma policy. Maharajadhiraja. But shared power with feudatories.
Council Mantriparishad: 3-12 ministers. Purohita, Senapati. Ministers existed, but less powerful. Kumaramatyas rose.
Departments 27 Adhyakshas. Sita, Panya, Suvarna. Arthashastra. Fewer departments. Many functions privatized via land grants.
Provinces 4 provinces. Governors = Kumaras (princes). Direct control. Provinces = Bhuktis. Governors = Uparikas. More autonomy.
Districts Vishaya/Ahara. Pradeshika (revenue) + Rajuka (justice). Paid officials. Vishaya. Vishayapati. Often hereditary, local landlords.
Villages Gramika headman. Gopa, Sthanika officials. State control. Village autonomy increased. Panchayats powerful.
Army Standing army. 6,00,000 infantry. Paid by state. Feudal army. Feudatories provided troops. Less central army.
Revenue Land revenue 1/4 to 1/6. Direct collection by state. Land grants to Brahmins/officials. They collected revenue. State lost.
Source Arthashastra, Indica, Ashokan Edicts Land grant inscriptions, Poona Plates, Fa-Hien

Don’t mug this table. Remember 1 line: “Mauryan = centralized bureaucracy, Gupta = decentralized feudalism.” Then pick 3 differences: King’s power, Land grants, Army. That’s enough for 20 marks.

The 7-Minute Comparison Framework

Structure: Intro → Similarities → Differences → Transition → Conclusion

1. Intro (30 sec): 1 line. “Mauryan (321-185 BCE) and Gupta (320-550 CE) admin represent shift from centralized bureaucracy to decentralized feudalism.”
2. Similarities (1 min): 2 points. Don’t skip this — UPSC gives marks for it.
→ Both: King at top, divine right theory.
→ Both: Provincial structure — Kumaras/Governors existed.
3. Differences (3.5 min): 3 points with sources. This is 60% of marks.
→ Centralization: Mauryan: 27 Adhyakshas, Arthashastra. Gupta: Fewer depts, functions to feudatories.
→ Land grants: Mauryan: State land, Sita farms. Gupta: Agrahara grants to Brahmins, Poona Plates.
→ Army: Mauryan: Standing army 6 lakh, Indica. Gupta: Feudal levies, no standing army.
4. Transition (1 min): 2 lines. Why change? “Mauryan over-centralization collapsed post-Ashoka. Gupta used feudalism for stability. Trade decline + Huna invasions forced decentralization.”
5. Conclusion (1 min): 1 line. “Shift from Mauryan bureaucracy to Gupta feudalism marked transition to medieval polity.”

Total: 7 minutes. 170 words. Integrated comparison. 15+/20.

The Full Answer: 2020 Q3(a) Solved

Q: Compare the administrative systems of Mauryas and Guptas. (20 marks)

Intro: Mauryan (321-185 BCE) and Gupta (320-550 CE) administrations represent shift from centralized bureaucracy to decentralized feudalism in ancient India.

Similarities: Both retained king as head with divine right — Chakravartin (Mauryan) and Maharajadhiraja (Gupta). Both had provincial structure: Kumaras governed provinces under Mauryas (Taxila, Ujjain), Uparikas under Guptas.

Differences: Mauryan administration was highly centralized with 27 Adhyakshas managing departments like Sita (crown lands), Panya (commerce) as per Arthashastra. Gupta had fewer departments, with functions delegated to feudatories via land grants. Mauryan state directly collected land revenue (1/4 to 1/6); Gupta made Agrahara grants to Brahmins who collected revenue, as seen in Poona Plates of Prabhavatigupta. Mauryan army was standing 6 lakh infantry (Indica); Gupta relied on feudal levies, with no permanent central army.

Transition: Mauryan over-centralization collapsed post-Ashoka due to communication limits. Gupta adopted feudalism for stability amid trade decline and Huna invasions. Land grants ensured loyalty and reduced administrative burden.

Conclusion: The shift from Mauryan bureaucracy to Gupta feudalism marked transition from ancient to early medieval polity, with decentralization and localism as key features.

Word count: 168 Time: 6 min 50 sec Marks: 15-17/20

Key Points to Remember

Mauryan = Bureaucracy: Salaried officials, direct state control, Arthashastra model. Think “modern IAS”.
Gupta = Feudalism: Land grants, hereditary officers, local autonomy. Think “medieval Zamindari”.
Transition Cause: Trade decline (Roman empire fell) + Huna invasions = State couldn’t pay bureaucracy. Solution: Land grants.
Sources: Mauryan: Arthashastra, Indica, Ashokan Edicts. Gupta: Land grant inscriptions, Fa-Hien. Cite 1 each.

What NOT to Write

Avoid these — they show you didn’t understand transition:

1. “Gupta was better than Mauryan” — UPSC doesn’t want value judgment. Say “different”, not “better”.
2. “Mauryan had spies, Gupta didn’t” — Irrelevant. Stick to structure: King, Council, Provinces, Revenue, Army.
3. “Gupta art was golden” — Question is on administration, not art. Don’t deviate.
4. Listing all 27 Adhyakshas — Write 2: Sita, Panya. Done.
5. “Feudalism started in Gupta” — Debatable. Say “feudal tendencies strengthened”. Nuance matters.

Bottom Line

UPSC doesn’t want two essays: “Mauryan admin was X. Gupta admin was Y.”

They want one integrated analysis: “Mauryan centralization declined to Gupta decentralization due to feudalism. Here’s how: King, Land, Army.”

Comparison > Description. Transition > Static. 7 minutes > 20 minutes.
