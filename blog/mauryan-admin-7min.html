How to Write Mauryan Administration in 7 Minutes

UPSC 2023 Q5(a), 2019 Q3(b), 2015 Q2(a). Same question, different years. Here’s the framework that works every time.

You see “Mauryan Administration” in the paper. Your brain dumps: Arthashastra, Indica, Ashokan edicts, 27 departments, Saptanga theory, Mantriparishad, Adhyakshas, Gopa, Sthanika...

You write 2 pages. You get 4/20.

Why? Because UPSC doesn’t want a data dump. They want: Structure. Sources. Limitation.

The examiner’s rubric: Can you organize Mauryan admin into Central→Provincial→District→Village? Can you cite 2 sources? Can you mention 1 limitation? If yes: 12+/20. If no: 4/20. Length doesn’t matter.

The 7-Minute Timer

Set your stopwatch. Stick to this:

0:00-1:00 Intro: 1 line. “Mauryan administration was highly centralized under king, assisted by council.” Done.
1:00-2:30 Central: King + Mantriparishad + 27 Adhyakshas. Source: Arthashastra. 1 line each.
2:30-4:00 Provincial: Kumaras/Governors in 4 provinces. Source: Ashokan edicts. 1 line.
4:00-5:30 District+Village: Pradeshikas, Rajukas, Gopa, Sthanika. Source: Indica. 1 line each.
5:30-6:30 Limitation: 1 line. “Sources exaggerate centralization; actual control limited by communication.”
6:30-7:00 Conclusion: 1 line. “Efficient for empire, but collapsed post-Ashoka due to over-centralization.”

Stop at 7:00. Even if you have more points. UPSC rewards completion, not volume.

The Central→Provincial→District→Village Framework

Memorize this structure. Fill it for any admin question:

1. Central Level

King: Head of state + judiciary + military. Chakravartin ideal.
Council: Mantriparishad — 3 to 12 ministers. Purohita, Senapati, Yuvaraja.
Departments: 27 Adhyakshas. Sita (crown lands), Panya (commerce), Suvarna (gold).
Source: Arthashastra Book 2.

2. Provincial Level

Divisions: 4 provinces — Uttarapatha, Avantiratha, Dakshinapatha, Kalinga.
Head: Kumara (prince) or Aryaputra (governor).
Source: Ashokan edicts mention Taxila, Ujjain, Tosali, Suvarnagiri.
Limitation: Princes often rebelled. Communication slow.

3. District Level

Head: Pradeshika (revenue), Rajuka (justice).
Unit: Ahara/Vishaya. 5-10 villages.
Source: Indica of Megasthenes + Ashokan edicts.
Limitation: Indica lost, known via Strabo/Arrian. May exaggerate.

4. Village Level

Head: Gramika (headman).
Officials: Gopa (5-10 villages), Sthanika (revenue district).
Source: Arthashastra Book 2 Ch 35.
Limitation: Village autonomy high. Central control weak beyond revenue.

The Full Answer: 2023 Q5(a) Solved in 7 Minutes

Q: Discuss the administrative system of the Mauryas. (10 marks)

Intro: Mauryan administration (321-185 BCE) was a centralized bureaucracy under the king, assisted by council and elaborate departmental system.

Central: King was head of state, judiciary, military — Chakravartin ideal. Assisted by Mantriparishad of 3-12 ministers including Purohita, Senapati. Arthashastra lists 27 Adhyakshas for departments like Sita (crown lands), Panya (commerce), Suvarna (gold). Source: Arthashastra Book 2.

Provincial: Empire divided into 4 provinces — Uttarapatha (Taxila), Avantiratha (Ujjain), Dakshinapatha (Suvarnagiri), Kalinga (Tosali). Headed by Kumaras or governors. Source: Ashokan edicts.

District: Provinces divided into Aharas/Vishayas under Pradeshikas (revenue) and Rajukas (justice). Source: Indica of Megasthenes.

Village: Lowest unit under Gramika. Gopa supervised 5-10 villages, Sthanika collected revenue. Source: Arthashastra Book 2 Ch 35.

Limitation: Sources like Arthashastra present ideal, not reality. Actual control limited by communication and distance. Indica is lost, known via secondary accounts.

Conclusion: Mauryan system was efficient for its time, enabling empire-building, but over-centralization contributed to decline post-Ashoka.

Word count: 152 Time: 6 min 45 sec Marks: 7-8/10

The 2 Sources + 1 Limitation Rule

UPSC doesn’t want 5 sources. They want 2 good sources + 1 limitation. This shows you read critically, not blindly.

Sources to Use (Pick 2):

Arthashastra (Kautilya): For departments, Adhyakshas, village admin. Book 2 is gold.
Ashokan Edicts: For provincial structure, Rajukas, Dhamma Mahamattas. Rock Edict 3, 4.
Indica (Megasthenes): For district admin, city admin, military. Cite via Strabo/Arrian.

Limitation to Mention (Pick 1):

Arthashastra: Normative text, not descriptive. Presents ideal, not reality.
Indica: Lost. Known only via secondary Greek accounts. May exaggerate.
Ashokan Edicts: Propagandist. Show Ashoka’s view, not full system.

What NOT to Write

Avoid These — They Waste Time:

Names of all 27 Adhyakshas. Write 3: Sita, Panya, Suvarna. Done.
Saptanga theory details. 1 line: “King, Amatya, Janapada, Durga, Kosa, Danda, Mitra.” Don’t explain each.
Salary tables. “Senapati got 48,000 panas” — irrelevant unless asked specifically.
Espionage system details. 1 line: “Spies under Gudhapurushas.” Don’t elaborate.
Comparison with Gupta. Question didn’t ask. Stick to Mauryan.
Historiography debate. “Romila Thapar vs Kosambi” — not for 10-markers.

Your 3-Day Revision Plan

Day 1: Memorize framework: Central→Provincial→District→Village. Write it 5 times.
Day 2: Add 2 sources to each level. Arthashastra + Edicts. Write full answer in 7 min.
Day 3: Add 1 limitation. Write answer again. Time yourself. If >7 min, cut details.

Total: 45 minutes. You now have Mauryan Admin ready for any paper.
